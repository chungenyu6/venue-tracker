## 💡 Strategic Notes

### Best Research ROI (for a PhD student)
| High-research-value path | What it unlocks |
|--------------------------|-----------------|
| NeurIPS / ICML / CVPR competition track (top-3) | Workshop extended abstract or full paper at top venue |
| ARC Prize — Paper Prize track ($50K) | High-profile research contribution in reasoning/agents |
| SWE-bench / HAL / GAIA leaderboard submission | Benchmark-track paper anchor for agent UQ work |
| NeurIPS EAI Challenge | Published results + embodied agent community visibility |

### Best Engineering Credibility Signals
| Competition | Why it matters |
|-------------|---------------|
| Kaggle Grandmaster | Long-term ranking signal — industry + academia both recognize it |
| MLPerf submission | Signals systems-level thinking (requires lab hardware) |
| OpenAI Parameter Golf | Model efficiency research — directly publishable angle |

### In-Person Networking Priorities
- **Berkeley Agentic AI Summit** (Aug): Highest-value networking for agent researchers — 2K+ in-person, industry + academia mix
- **Sky Camp** (Nov): Systems + AI intersection, Berkeley ecosystem; low barrier to attend
- **NeurIPS competition workshop** (Dec): Physically presenting a top result here = field-wide visibility

### Key Practical Notes
- **ARC Prize Paper Track**: The $50K paper prize is designed for novel research contributions — not just a strong leaderboard score. Frame as a research paper with theoretical grounding and submit to the prize concurrently with a venue paper
- **NeurIPS competitions**: Registration typically opens in June–August; results/workshop presentations in December. Many run on Kaggle — monitor the NeurIPS competition blog each summer
- **Conference competition tracks**: Always check accepted workshop lists at ICLR/ICML/NeurIPS/CVPR each cycle — specific tracks change year to year. Filter for: agent benchmarks, VLM evaluation, reasoning, uncertainty
- **Leaderboards (HAL, GAIA, SWE-bench)**: Submitting a novel method and writing a technical report is often sufficient for workshop papers or findings papers — do not treat these as purely engineering exercises
- **Berkeley events**: RDI and Sky Lab events are invite-lean but announce public registration — watch their mailing lists and Twitter/X accounts
- Always verify dates on official sites — timelines shift 1–4 weeks year to year
