## 💡 Strategic Notes

### Open Application vs. Nomination Required
| Self-apply directly | Must go through UF / advisor |
|--------------------|------------------------------|
| Meta, NVIDIA, NSF GRFP, DOE CSGF, NDSEG, SMART, Hertz, Open Phil, Two Sigma, Jane Street | Google PhD, Amazon, Apple, IBM, Adobe, Microsoft, Qualcomm, Intel |

### Key Practical Notes
- **Google PhD Fellowship**: UF's internal nomination deadline is typically 2–3 weeks before Google's official mid-May date — reach out to UF grad school in early April each year
- **NSF GRFP**: The eligibility window is strictly limited. You can apply at most twice total (once as a senior undergrad, once in PhD year 1 or 2). After year 2, permanently ineligible
- **DOE CSGF**: The mandatory 3-month DOE lab practicum is non-negotiable — it provides large-scale HPC access (ORNL, NERSC) that complements HiPerGator for scaling experiments
- **Meta Fellowship**: Fully open — no nomination barrier. One of the highest-value fellowships you can apply to without advisor involvement
- **Hertz**: ~3% acceptance, multi-round interviews. Worth attempting but don't count on it — apply in parallel with NSF GRFP
- **Open Phil**: Their "potential risks from advanced AI" priority explicitly covers hallucination, reliability, and robustness — frame your UQ/robustness research in those terms
- **US citizenship requirement**: NSF GRFP, DOE CSGF, NDSEG, SMART, Hertz all require US citizenship or PR — verify your status before investing time
- Always verify deadlines on official sites — most shift by 1–3 weeks each year
