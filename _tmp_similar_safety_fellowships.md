# Similar AI Safety / Alignment Fellowships — Quick List

> **Temp file** — for review before adding any to tracker.
> Focused on: funded, research-oriented, graduate/researcher level, not undergrad-only.

---

## Tier 1 — Highest Fit & Prestige

### [Anthropic Fellows Program](https://alignment.anthropic.com/2025/anthropic-fellows-program-2026/)
- **Duration:** 4 months | **Stipend:** ~$3,850/week + ~$15K/month compute
- **Next deadline:** ~April 26, 2026 (July cohort) — **may already be closed; check**
- **Format:** Remote (two 2026 cohorts: May & July start)
- **Focus:** Scalable oversight, adversarial robustness/AI control, mechanistic interpretability, AI security
- **Fit:** Excellent — robustness + agent control + empirical safety is your zone. 40%+ of fellows get full-time Anthropic offers.

### [MATS (ML Alignment & Theory Scholars)](https://www.matsprogram.org/)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks + possible 6-month extension | **Stipend:** $15K + $12K compute + housing + meals
- **Location:** Berkeley or London (in-person)
- **Focus:** Technical alignment, interpretability, governance — flexible track selection
- **Fit:** Good — mentor-matching means you can target an agent safety or UQ-relevant mentor. Best for building alignment research credibility.

### [Vitalik Buterin PhD Fellowship (Future of Life Institute)](https://futureoflife.org/grant-program/phd-fellowships/)
- **Duration:** 5 years (PhD funding) | **Stipend:** $40K/yr + tuition + $10K research fund
- **Next deadline:** ~November 2026 (annual cycle; 2025 deadline was Nov 21)
- **PhD required:** Yes — this one is specifically for PhD students
- **Focus:** AI existential safety
- **Fit:** Strong if you frame UQ/robustness as existential-risk-relevant. Rare program that funds a full PhD around AI safety — high value if you qualify.

## Tier 2 — Solid, Worth Considering

### [ERA Fellowship (Cambridge)](https://erafellowship.org/)
- **Duration:** 10 weeks | **Stipend:** ~£34K/yr prorated + accommodation + meals + travel
- **Location:** Cambridge, UK (in-person)
- **Focus:** Mitigating risks from frontier AI; topic-flexible within that scope
- **Fit:** Good — flexible enough to cover your work. Strong UK AI safety ecosystem exposure.
- **Note:** Summer 2026 applications closed; watch for Fall 2026 cycle.

### [CBAI Summer Research Fellowship](https://www.cbai.ai/summer-research-fellowship-26)
- **Duration:** 9 weeks (June 8 – Aug 10, 2026) | **Stipend:** $10K + up to $10K compute + housing + meals
- **Location:** Cambridge, MA (in-person; Harvard/MIT ecosystem)
- **Deadline:** April 12, 2026 — **already passed for 2026**; apply next cycle
- **Focus:** Interpretability, multi-agent safety, formal verification, risk management frameworks
- **Fit:** Good — multi-agent safety directly relevant.

### [Pivotal Research Fellowship](https://www.pivotal-research.org/fellowship)
- **Duration:** 9 weeks (+ ~70% of fellows get 6-month extension) | **Stipend:** £6–8K + meals + travel + compute
- **Location:** London (in-person)
- **Focus:** Technical AI safety, AI governance/policy, AI-bio; mentors from Google DeepMind, Redwood Research
- **Fit:** Moderate-good — technical track fits; London location may or may not work.

### [Global AI Safety Fellowship (Impact Academy)](https://globalaisafetyfellowship.com/)
- **Duration:** Up to 6 months flexible | **Stipend:** Up to $30K
- **Format:** Remote / placements at CHAI (UCB), FAR.AI, Mila, UK AISI, Conjecture
- **Focus:** Broad AI safety; placement-based
- **Fit:** Moderate — placement at CHAI (Berkeley) would be high fit. Watch for next application round.

## Tier 3 — Supplementary / Part-Time Options

### [SPAR (Supervised Program for Alignment Research)](https://sparai.org/)
- **Duration:** 12 weeks part-time (5–40 hrs/week) | **Stipend:** Unclear — primarily mentorship program
- **Format:** Remote; 130+ projects available (Spring 2026)
- **Focus:** All AI safety tracks; mentors from DeepMind, RAND, Apollo, UK AISI, etc.
- **Fit:** Low intensity — good if you want a mentored side project alongside PhD. Not a funding vehicle.

### [CAIS AI & Society Fellowship](https://safe.ai/fellowship)
- **Duration:** 3 months | **Location:** San Francisco (in-person)
- **PhD required:** Yes (or equivalent)
- **Focus:** Societal impacts of advanced AI — economics, law, policy, international relations
- **Fit:** Lower fit for technical ML work — more suited for a policy/governance pivot.

### [IAPS AI Policy Fellowship](https://www.iaps.ai/fellowship)
- **Duration:** ~12 weeks | **Stipend:** $15–22K | **Location:** D.C. residency + remote project
- **Focus:** AI policy research
- **Fit:** Low for core ML research, but worth knowing if you develop a governance angle.

## Funding (Not Fellowships, But Relevant)

### [Long-Term Future Fund (LTFF)](https://funds.effectivealtruism.org/funds/far-future)
- Grants ~$25K median for individuals — career transitions, upskilling, independent research
- Rolling applications, no institutional affiliation required
- Good bridge funding between academic positions or for side research projects

### [Survival & Flourishing Fund (SFF)](https://survivalandflourishing.fund/)
- Grants for AI safety projects; $34.9M distributed in 2025
- "Speculation grants" with decisions in ~1 week for smaller asks
- Less structured than a fellowship — apply for a specific project
